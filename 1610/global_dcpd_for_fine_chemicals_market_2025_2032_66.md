## Market Overview
Dicyclopentadiene (DCPD) is a versatile chemical intermediate primarily used in the production of hydrocarbon resins, adhesives, inks, and coatings. Its high reactivity, compatibility with various substrates, and ability to enhance product performance make it a preferred choice in multiple industries. The growing demand for high-performance resins in automotive, construction, and packaging applications is a key growth driver.

## Regional Analysis

The region benefits from advanced manufacturing capabilities and stringent environmental regulations that favor high-purity DCPD. The presence of key players like DowDuPont and ExxonMobil Chemical further strengthens the market.

**Europe:** Europe is a significant consumer of DCPD, driven by the automotive and construction sectors. Germany and France lead the demand due to their robust chemical manufacturing base. **Middle East &amp; Africa:** This region shows moderate growth, primarily due to petrochemical industry expansion in GCC countries. However, limited downstream applications and reliance on imports restrict market potential.

## End-User Industry Analysis
The **resin industry** is the largest consumer of DCPD, accounting for over 40% of global demand. DCPD-based resins are widely used in automotive composites, marine coatings, and construction materials due to their durability and chemical resistance. The **paint and coating industry** follows closely, leveraging DCPD for high-performance formulations requiring UV stability and adhesion properties.

The **adhesive sector** uses DCPD in hot-melt and pressure-sensitive adhesives, driven by packaging and automotive assembly applications. The **ink industry** employs DCPD-derived hydrocarbon resins to enhance print quality and drying time. Additionally, the **pharmaceutical and agrochemical industries** are emerging as niche consumers, using high-purity DCPD as a building block for active ingredients.

Quantitatively, the resin segment is projected to grow at a CAGR of 3.5%, while adhesives and paint are expected to grow at 3.0% and 2.8%, respectively. The Asia-Pacific region dominates end-user consumption, contributing over 50% of global demand, followed by North America (25%) and Europe (20%).
